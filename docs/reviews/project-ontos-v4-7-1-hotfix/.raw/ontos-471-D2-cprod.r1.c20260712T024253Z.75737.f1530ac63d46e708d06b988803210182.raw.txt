Review written to `docs/reviews/project-ontos-v4-7-1-hotfix/D.2-claude-product.md`. Verdict: **approve-with-comments**.

**The hotfix's central product claim holds under direct test.** I exercised the two recovery behaviors rather than reading them: a `verify` against a file with invalid UTF-8 bytes prints `❌ docs/bad.md is not valid UTF-8 and was not modified. Re-save the file as UTF-8, then retry.`, exits 1, and the file's checksum is unchanged afterward. Running `ontos log` twice with the same title refuses the second with a message naming the path and offering two concrete outs, and the earlier log survives. Refusals are fail-closed, non-destructive, and legible.

**Schema-3.4 automation contracts are intact.** Both failures return a `"schema_version": "3.4"` envelope with the right error codes (`E_COMMAND_FAILED`, `E_FILE_EXISTS`) and `exit_code: 1` matching the real process exit. I specifically checked stream separation, since the `❌` line appears alongside `--json` output — the decorated line goes to stderr and the envelope to stdout, so `ontos --json verify … 2>/dev/null | jq` works cleanly. Not a bug.

**The one finding worth tracking is a failure-visibility gap, not a correctness one.** When `[paths].logs_dir` first takes effect, `ontos log` writes to the new directory, leaves old logs where they are, and prints only a success line — no mention that history is now split across two directories, and no warning if the configured directory falls outside scan scope (where logs quietly drop out of maps and queries). The CHANGELOG and Manual are candid about this, but documentation is a weak notification for the user most exposed: the one upgrading who won't re-read the Manual. The `warnings: []` envelope field already exists to carry this at no cost to the schema contract. I filed it as Medium; two Low copy nits (no concrete `iconv`-style recovery command; `log --help` omits the exclusivity rule) round out the issues.

Worth calling out as a positive: `a71ac4a` also fixes a Manual claim that its own change had invalidated — the old text said pushes "append to the same log," which exclusivity makes false. That class of thing usually ships broken.

Two honesty notes on coverage, both recorded in the review: I read but did not execute the `promote` and `migrate` refusal paths within the timebox — their diffs look correct, and `migrate --apply` pre-scans before planning any write, which is the stronger ordering, but I flagged batch-`promote`'s mid-batch abort as worth a closer look by someone with more budget. And the scan-scope consequence in issue 1 is inferred from the Manual's own caveat plus the absence of any warning in `log.py`; I did not run `ontos map`/`query` to observe it, per the no-activation constraint.
