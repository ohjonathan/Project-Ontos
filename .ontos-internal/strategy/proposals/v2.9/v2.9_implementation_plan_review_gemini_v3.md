## 1. Final Assessment

This is my third and final review of the v2.9 implementation plan. The evolution of this plan from v1.0.0 to v1.2.0 has been exemplary. The Chief Architect (Claude) has demonstrated a remarkable ability to integrate complex, multi-source feedback into a coherent, robust, and actionable strategy.

The plan is now exceptionally thorough. The addition of the "Changes in v1.2.0" section, which explicitly maps new changes to their source, is a model of transparency and effective collaboration.

All of my significant concerns from the previous two rounds of review have been addressed:

*   The security and atomicity of the `install.py` process have been significantly hardened with the external `checksums.json`, the sentinel pattern, and clearer documentation of the threat model.
*   The Curation Level UX is now much stronger with the addition of the interactive `promote` command and the `.ontosignore` file.
*   The decision to defer `ruamel.yaml` for comment preservation, while a trade-off I would have made differently, is now a well-documented and accepted design choice in service of the zero-dependency goal for v2.x. I respect the consistency of this principle.

The plan is not just a list of features; it is a well-orchestrated set of engineering tasks, complete with risk analysis, testing strategies, and clear success criteria.

## 2. Final Verdict

The plan is ready for implementation. It has my full endorsement.

**Verdict: Unconditional Approval.**
